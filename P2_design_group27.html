Our service

Mediafy is a service created by co-founders William Østensen, Victor Jørgensen and Erling Olweus. All three authors are students at NTNU in the course IT2805 Webteknologi. The idea behind Mediafy is to let the user explore new music, movies and TV shows.

Purpose and goals

Mediafy's purpose is to deliver top recommendations of the best music, movies and TV-shows to media enthusiats. The goal of Mediafy is to recommend trending music, media and TV, editors picks and IMDB recommendations while giving people a easy-to-use and clean site to learn about the newest within music, movies and series.

Navigation structure

The navigation structure of Mediafy's website is overall hierarchial; The mainpage is at the top and have access to all the subpages and some of the articles featured on the frontpage (top stories). Next we have the subpages (music, film, series and about). These can reach eachother and the mainpage in a linear fashion through the navigation bar/meny. In addition the subpages will have different articles concerning each of these topics(exception in the about page which will only have information about the company). These different articles about distinct topics within music, film and series can only be reached from the respective subpage. That means that you can not click directly into an article about music from the film subpage, you'll have to go through the music subpage. From these articles you will be able to click directly into the different subpages and mainpage via the navbar, but you cannot however go directly from one article to another one in a linear fashion regardless of what subpage the article belongs to. This is to clearly separate which topic the different articles belongs to and give the site and easy to use and understand structure.

Page Layout and Appearance

In making the decision about our page layout and design we wanted to focus on our goal to make the website have a clean and dark look with a sort of "vintage"-vibe. Therefore we chose to use a dark blue background (#28546C) with a very dark blue/light black article background and black, white and light gray for nabber, footer and texts complementing these colors. We also wanted to focus on cool imagery and titles that helps the design of the page reach these goals. With this use of colors and images and trendy look we appeal to the target audience (younger users). This is also the reason for the modern and easy-look on the navbar. For fonts we will use Garamond for titles and Helvetica(Neue) for article texts.

Minimum requirements

To fulfil P2's minimum requirements we have planned to make four distinct JavaScript applications.

  • slider.js
    • This script will be the application to run the slider for our images on the frontpage, and other subpages a slider might be used.
  • randomPicks.js
    • As described in the previous sections, on the frontpage and the main pages for all three categories we will have top picks, editor picks and weekly picks. To avoid always showing the same images with link to the same article, this application will randomly pick e.g. one of the top picks and show the article image as an clickable link.
  • subscribe.js
    • Name of the script says it all. This script will mock the funciton of a "Subscribe to our newsletter" button which will be placed in the footer of every page. The script will print something along the lines of "Thank you for subscribing" to the person browsing, without actually signing up the user.
  • coverAnimation.js
    • This script is somewhat more advanced than the other three. When hovering an album by an artist, the idea is that an LP will slide out from the album cover and display some more information about hte album / artist.
